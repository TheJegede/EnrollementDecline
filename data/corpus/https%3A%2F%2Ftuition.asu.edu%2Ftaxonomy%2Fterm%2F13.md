Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grants (IASG) are for undergraduate students who are not Federal Pell Grant eligible; whose parent or guardian died as a result of military service in Iraq or Afghanistan after September 11, 2001; and who, at the time of the parent's or guardian's death, were less than 24 years old or were enrolled at least part time at an institution of higher education.

**Only complete this form if you have a To Do item listed in My ASU.** This online form is required when a student is a financial aid recipient** and** has initiated a complete withdrawal. Submissions must be made within 5 days of initiating a withdrawal.

## Purpose

Short-term loans are available through the Short-Term Loan Program for students experiencing emergency financial situations. Short-term loans are also available for books if a financial aid disbursement is delayed. A short-term loan is typically $500 per semester. If a student has greater need, we will review those on a case-by-case basis.

## Eligibility requirements

You must be a currently enrolled student at Arizona State University and be admitted to a degree-seeking or certificate program.

Consortium agreements enable ASU degree-seeking students to participate in financial aid programs while concurrently attending ASU and another accredited higher education institution. ASU reserves the right to not enter into a consortium agreement if, in the opinion of ASU's Financial Aid and Scholarship Services, that consortium agreement would be administratively prohibitive. **Consortium agreements assist in receiving additional financial aid, not for the deferment of loans.**
